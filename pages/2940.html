Additional Information

Although he thinks the internet is important in making the artworld, and artworks, visible, he believes that it is crucial not to buy art online. He believes art should be seen up close, to see its texture, its color, as seeing a work online distorts it and one cannot get a proper sense of its qualiy.
He believes that, although a piece of art may have an immediate impact on one, one should consider it properly before buying it; as art, for him, should have some sort of longevity and only on reflection can one acertain whether or not the work has depth and will stand the test of time.
He is one of a group of experts for the 'School for Creative Startups', an online course which seeks to provide the business know-how and advice that creative people need to build sustainable startups.
He qualifies his collection as eclectic, and sees every work as embodying a memory, a reminder of where he was and what his taste was like at a particular moment in time.
His favourite work of art is Caspar David Friedrich's `The Wanderer above the Sea of Clouds´, as he feels it reflects his entrepreneurial dreams. His favourite era of artworks is Dutch Old Masters.
His passion for art started at school, thanks to an inspirational art teacher. He then went on to buy his first artwork in Moscow in 1990. He has since amassed quite a collection, having at one time bought an artwork from every art fair he went to, which has resulted in his having to keep a number of them in storage.
The most he has ever spent on an artwork is £5,000.

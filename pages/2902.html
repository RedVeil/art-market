Additional Information

Emfietzoglou bought “????? ???????” (The Secret School) by Nikolaos Gysi at a Christie's auction in 1993 for 187.5 million Drachmas.
He is often given advice by art historians Anna Kafetzi and Effie Strousa. Ms. Strousa is author of the books and essays published about the collection and curator of the collection.
In 1999 a catalog that documented the Emphietsoglou collection was published in Greek and English (Strousa,Effi: The Emfietzoglou collection, 1999).
Prodromos Emphietsoglou established an art museum in Athens. He gave his private art collection of over 500 works to the public. These days the Emphietsoglou gallery offers a review of 750 works of modern Greek art including some of the best paintings from the last 200 years. Emphietsoglou still collects privately.
Prodromos Emphietsoglou published "The Emfietzoglou Collection: Modern and Contemporary Greek Art". It is a copious volume that follows the history of Greek art from the late 19th century to present times through the holdings of a private collection.
The Emfietzoglou family collects art since the 19th century. They were particularly interested in Greek painters.Their passion in combination with many artists' donations led in 1999 to the foundation of the museum.
The Emphietsoglou collection consists only of Greek art.
The first artwork he bought was "Acropolis - House Parthenis, 1970-1973" by the Greek painter Spyros Vassiliou.

Additional Information

After thirteen years in banking, she made a career change in 2003 and volunteered at the Sherman Galleries in Sydney; an experience which deepened her insight and involvement with the arts.
If there is an artist she really likes, she tries to buy an artwork from the start, middle and end of their career.
In 2005, her most expensive purchase was just under half a million pounds.
She has a keen sense of collecting as a way of contributing to the art world, not just as a way of accumulating artworks. It's in this vein that she launched 'Incubator', a company which acts as a creative platform, through which artworks are commissioned, funded and exhibited.
She is both the youngest collector and youngest art donor in Australia.
She is particularly interested in New Media, such as Photography or Video, and in collecting artworks by emerging artists. She also tends to collect mediums she has never seen before.
She keeps the first edition of each design which Incubator commissions or funds.
She no longer buys works instantaneously, but gives herself two or three days to think over it, research the piece, see where the artist is in his or her career, before making an informed decision.
She started collecting Australian New Media in 2002, although she had already been collecting artworks for almost ten years.

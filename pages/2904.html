Additional Information

Although his focus is predominantly on Middle Eastern art, he states that his collecting habits cannot be specialized in it completely, as he loves art, as a whole, too much.
He deplores the effects of globalisation on the art world in which Middle Eastern art seeks to imitate Western styles. He admires Middle Eastern artists who manage to assert themselves and establish their own style without this imitative tendency; artists such as Fateh Moudares, Louai Kayali, Elias Zayat and Hamed Nada.
He especially enjoys discovering new art and meeting artists, something he got to do whilst working at his gallery `FenÂ´ in Berlin.
He will only buy an artwork if he feels he has had or felt an immediate connection or interest in it.
His Berlin gallery 'Fen' specialized in Syrian furniture and Modern Art from the Middle East and Northern Africa.
In reference to his belief in the globalising tendencies within the art market, he admires the works of Modern Chinese artists, believing theirs is a good example of how artists can develop their own, unique style and a modern approach to art.
When looking at a work of art, it is important for him to know something on the artist, as he likes to understand the context in which the work was created.

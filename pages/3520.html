Additional Information

AVA's aim is to build a contemporary art collection by commissioning, rather than simply buying, works from the artists. Based on a two-year contract with each artist, they guarantee to keep some of the works for five years.
It was through La Placa's partner, and Platt's ex-girlfriend, Fiamma Montagu, that they met. This partnership is based on Platt's financial directorship and La Placa's creative directorship.
The AVA Collection is mainly based on works it funds, and is displayed in a 5,000 sq ft former bus garage, previously home to the Almeida Theatre, near King’s Cross.
They have also held exhibitions in various locations, including 'The Age of the Marvelous' (2009) in the former Holy Trinity Church in Marylebone; and 'Vanitas: The Transience of Earthly Pleasures' (2010) in the former Sierra Leone Embassy, staged during Frieze Art Fair.
Unlike most galleries, which focus on selling the works, AVA's main interest is in working with the artists directly.

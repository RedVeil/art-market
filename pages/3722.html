Additional Information

He believes it is important to keep the art of portraiture going, saying that, to do so, one must raise oneself above the 'vanity threshold', for a portrait is the expression of the artist rather than that of the sitter. He himself owns several portraits of himself and his family.
He has been the official art adviser to the House of Commons and to the House of Lords (1988-2011).
He is a regular sponsor of exhibitions and projects, such as the Royal Cornwall Museum, the Falmouth Art Gallery and the Walker Art Gallery, Liverpool.
He is also a writer and television presenter for the BBC's Antiques Road Show and, most especially, for the BBC's 'Fake or Fortune?' series with Fiona Bruce.
His interest in portraits comes from his fascination with faces, with their potential for theatricality, atmosphere and gesture, as well as that of costume and expression. He regularly moves his paintings around in order to alter the atmosphere of a room.
They also own a cabinet of curiosities, which includes miniature Chinese shoes and an 18th-century oval leather case with the picture of a girl, as well as Catherine Mould's Victorian Staffordshire figures.

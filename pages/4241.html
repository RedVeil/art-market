Additional Information

He established in 2008 a foundation with Global Impact for the promotion and development of Nigerian art and culture.
He is an avid art collector who arguably has the largest private collections of contemporary artists works both Nigerian and non-Nigerian artists.
His official name is Prince Omooba Yemisi Adedoyin Shyllon.
In 2009, he commenced an OYASAF fellowship grant for non-Nigerian art and culture scholars from the five continents of the world to visit and interact with their Nigerian counterparts and research into Nigerian art and culture. So far, some 12 scholars from three continents have benefitted under the programme.
The Onoba Yemisi Adedoyin shyllon Art Foundation (OYASAF), regularly loans out artworks for exhibitions in local and international museums e.g. The New York Museum of African Art, USA.
The University of Port Harcourt honored the engineer-turned-cultural philanthropist with an Honorary Doctor of Letters Degree (D.Litt.).
Prince Yemisi Shyllon is planning to open a private art museum in Lagos
In 2014, Prince Yemisi Shyllon and the Pan-Atlantic University began plans for the creation of a university museum hosting works from his collection, with an initial donation of 1,000 artworks plus a committment for donating funds towards the construction of the museum building and its long-term sustainability. The museum was named the Yemisi Shyllon Museum of Art (YSMA) and is expected to open to the public in 2020.
After being confronted by a number of people telling him he was worshiping his artworks, Shyllon installed a large cross at the front of his house to indicate his Christian faith.

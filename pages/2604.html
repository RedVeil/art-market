Additional Information

He collects works by 'people who are making art, but are not thinking about making art'; by undiscovered and untrained artists from the 19th, 20th and 21st centuries.
He does not like the term 'outsider', but prefers the use of the term 'non-traditional' when looking at such art.
His endeavour is supported by many Contemporary artists, including Damian Hirst and Cindy Sherman, with a number of them collaborating on his exhibitions and catalogues, such as Sir Peter Blake on 'Exhibition #3'.
The Museum of Everything also actively promotes the artists it represents, both as an exhibitor and an archive.
The Museum of Everything is a touring museum and has held exhibitions at Tate Modern, the Agnelli Museum, Selfridges and the Garage Center of Contemporary Culture.
What attracts him to collect 'outsider art' is the sense of privacy and intimacy which this art evokes. He contrasts this to the contemporary art market in which artists rely on their audience's, and the art market's, expectations.

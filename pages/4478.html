Additional Information

"Pinto" as the name of the museum represents "door" to artists as well as visitors. It also represents a portal to young artists and the door to the world of Filippino contemporary art.
He and Antonio Leaño worked on designing and opening the new wing of the museum which is 1,000 sqm. This new wing was opened in December 2012.
He started his own collection after having read a book-piece on Antipolo.
He worked as an art director in "Kulit Bulilit”, Channel 13 of children show in the 80's. He also worked as a graphic artist in Botak sportswear.
His own museum, Pinto Art Museum, was designed by Antonio Leaño who also designed his own private villa and his Pino Art Gallery. This museum took 2 years to complete.
Most painting being exhibited are by the artist Emmanuel Garibay.
Unlike other collectors, he does believe in keeping his collection private.

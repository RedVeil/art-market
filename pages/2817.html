Additional Information

Although she enjoys living alongside the artworks she owns, she is also keen on lending them to exhibitions, believing it is important for those artists to be seen and appreciated.
As regarding her collecting choices, she has become braver over the years, not only in her taste, but also in how much she is ready to spend.
For her, establishing a relationship with the artist is more of an added, rather than an essential, criterion.
She also owns the painting 'The Patron Saint of Sculpture: Construction in a Sphere' (1919), Acrylic on canvas, 310 x 310 mm.
She has bought a number of the works she owns from the Ceri Hand Gallery, Manchester Contemporary and the John Moores Painting Prize.
When giving advice on collecting, she believes that one should buy what one likes, set aside a certain sum for collecting every year, and talk to galleries about the artists and artworks they are displaying. She also recommends becoming a member of the Contemporary Art Society.

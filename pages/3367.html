Additional Information

After a trip to China in 2004, he became particularly interested in Chinese characters. This interest in 'the aesthetics of the signs' led him to study and photograph them, thus building up a collection on which to base his own works.
From 1976, he taught at the Royal College of Art, where he was first Professor of Painting (1986-1998) and then Professor Emeritus. He was also a visiting lecturer at Glasgow School of Art.
He was also a member of the advisory panels for the Arts Council and the Serpentine Gallery; Chairman of the Tate's Exhibitions Committee, and Chairman of the Art & Design Research Exercise for the Higher Education Funding Council for England.

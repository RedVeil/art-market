Additional Information

He enjoys buying artwork from auction houses. He bids anonymously, word of his purchases sometimes gets out through various dealer friends.
He is one of the top 200 collectors worldwide.
In 2006 he bought Yves Klein’s blue monochrome RE 9-1, from 1961, for £3.7 million.
In 2007 he bought a side table by André Groult for €600,000.
In 2009, Sotheby's organized an auction in cooperation with Mr. Mavromatis with furniture from his London house. The auction was named The Dimitri Mavromatis Collection: Important French Furniture and Sèvres Porcelain from the Chester Square Residence, London; a catalogue by the same name was published by Sotheby's.
In June 2011 he bought Picassos portrait 'Femme assise, robe bleue', from 1939, for £18 million at Christie’s London.
In June 2011 he paid around $28 million for one of Picasso's famous paintings of Dora Maar at a Christie's Action.
Mavromatis bought Picasso`s "Femme assise au chapeau" at Frieze London for $7 million.
Mavromatis is a Greek art collector based in Paris who made his fortune in investment banking and asset management.
Mr. Mavrommatis bought a pair of "Gonse" armchairs by the Art Deco master Émile-Jacques Ruhlmann for $1.4 million at a Phillips de Pury auction.
Mr. Mavrommatis has one of the strongest private collections of Sèvres porcelain in the world.

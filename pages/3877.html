Additional Information

He is the grandson of Irish immigrants, his family is from County Kerry, Ireland.
He founded an American Law Library at University College, Cork in honor of his late father, the Hon. John J. Burns.
He has one of the largest collections of Irish art.
He is an art collector and philanthropist.
He was also a principal benefactor of the first Irish Famine memorial in Cambridge, Massachusetts, which was dedicated in July, 1997 by former Irish President Mary Robinson.
He was the leading fundraiser behind the effort to restore the world famous Marsh’s Library at St. Patrick’s Close in Dublin, the oldest public library in Ireland.
In 1986 he established The Honorable John J. Burns Library of Rare Books and Special Collections at Boston College, in honor of his father.
Literature: A Review of America's Eye: Irish Paintings from the Collection of Brian P. Burns, Kate Robinson, Studies: An Irish Quarterly Review, Vol. 86, No. 341 (Spring, 1997), pp. 78-80, Published by: Irish Province of the Society of Jesus
Mr. Burns became the youngest director of the American Irish Foundation, established in 1963 by then-President John F. Kennedy and Ireland’s President Eamon de Valera.

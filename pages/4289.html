Additional Information

He bought his first major artwork, a painting by the artist Marc Séguin, with part of the 8000$ inherited from his grandfather.
He discovered the work of the artist Marc Séguin early on, and has made a point of acquiring a work from each of his series.
He has said that he would never sell his artworks; that they are not investments but a 'conversion' into something above and beyond money, into something which survives civilisations. He believes art shows how one viewed life at a particular moment in time.
He houses his private collection within his company's headquarters, which act as a small museum of sorts; and which had, until 2007, been home to the Musée Marc-Aurèle Fortin.
He sees art as standing as the testament of a civilisation, as a bottle thrown to the sea for future generations. As such, he does not hesitate to buy artworks which are difficult to comprehend, as he believes this is what helps a civilisation to progress, to conquer its fears.
He started collecting 10 years ago and has invested half of his company's profits into his art collecting.
He started with a budget of 8000$ and has stated that he prefers to invest in art rather than buying the latest car model.
His collection, as well as that he has put together within his company Giverny Capital, brings together the works of 70 artists, of which over 80% are from Quebec.
His dream is to build a museum in order to share his collection with future generations. If this dream does not take shape, he wishes to donate his collection to the Musée des Beaux-Arts de Montréal.
His love of art began when he saw Claude Monet's 'La Cathédrale de Rouen' at the Musée d’Orsay in 1987. From this initial love of Impressionism, he went on to discover Abstraction. Minimalism and Contemporary art.

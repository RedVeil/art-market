Additional Information

In 2012 Uli Sigg announced that he would donate 1463 artworks to the M+ (Museum of Visual Culture) in Hong Kong. Furthermore, he sold 47 works for $22 Million to the museum. He believes this is the best move for the collection so that the art may stay in China but have maximum freedom to be displayed.
Uli Sigg is not dependent on the instruction of experts, emphasizing the process of collecting, and gathering artworks in person. In fact, the decades he has spent collecting art himself has made him an expert.
Uli Sigg's Collection is the largest and most complete collection system concerning contemporary Chinese art in the world.
In 2019, Uli Sigg founded The Sigg Prize, a biennial award that recognises outstanding practices of artists born or working in the Greater China region. He also founded the Chinese Contemporary Art Award in 1998.
Uli Sigg enjoys the process of commissioning artworks.

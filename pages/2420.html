Additional Information

Although she believes that collecting according to a theme is too restrictive, she does believe that one's collection should be coherent in terms of styles and time.
Fatima and Eskander Maleki do not buy a work of art to re-sell it, but because they love the piece. They research every work, including the artist and the genre, very carefully before buying. They place a lot of importance on the meaning behind the work.
Fatima Maleki began collecting old masters before moving on to contemporary art; a passion derived from her grandparents, who collected Persian manuscripts, and her parents, themselves collectors of old masters and antiques.
She has been on the Contemporary Art Society's Annual Fundraiser Committee in 2013, 2012, 2010 and 2009.
She sees a collection as a whole, rather than simply the sum of its parts; something with its own story, made up from the various experiences which have shaped it into what it is.
They hosted the Prix Pictet event (2008) and a reception for the Magic of Persia Contemporary Art Prize (2008) at their home.

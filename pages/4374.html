Additional Information

As well as collecting art, he has commissioned a number of artworks; commissions which have come from his close relationships with artists.
He displays his collection on his walls and rotates the artworks every two years. He displays them thematically and keeps a room for storage.
He is particularly touched by art which is socially engaged; by artworks which make one think without necessarily providing any answers; which transform and make us more human by making us aware of our fundamental tensions.
He regularly visits the Clark, Esse and Articule Galleries.
He sees the art one collects as a witness to what one is living, as artists speak to one of the society in which one lives.
His first art purchase was for the sum of US$ 3,000.
If the time comes when a work in his collection speaks to him less, he will sell it, as he did with a bronze by Joe Fafard and a print by Riopelle; although this rarely happens.
With time, certain themes have emerged within his collection, themes which deal with the human condition in the 21st c.: faith and spirituality; eroticism and sexuality; environment and nature; money, power and wealth; violence and war; solitude and alienation.

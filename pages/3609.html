Additional Information

She donated over 400 fashion items in 2013 to the Design Museum, of which she is a trustee. It is the museum's first ever fashion acquisition, and includes pieces by Chanel, Givenchy and Vivienne Westwood.
She has also been involved on the board of the Garden Museum; as a member of the Wallace Collection's International Council; was Chairman of Tate's 'Patrons of New Art' Council; as well as on the judging panels for the RIBA Awards, RIBA Manser Medal, Turner Prize and Arts in Business Awards.
She has played a vital role in her husband's selection of works by young British artists, such as Damien Hirst, Fiona Ray, Michael Craig, Antony Gormley and Langlands & Bell, for his British Land offices and public spaces.
She is especially known as a contemporary fashion collector and donated over 1000 pieces to the V&A in 1997, now known as the Jill Ritblat Collection; some of which were displayed in the One Womans Wardrobe exhibition (1998-99). This collection covers pieces of couture and ready-to-wear from the 1960s, and includes pieces by Giorgio Armani, Celia Birtwell, Ossie Clark, Herms, Yves Saint Laurent and Bellville Sassoon.

Additional Information

In 1962 Ian Vorres wrote a highly successful book: The Last Grand Duchess. It was the authorized biography of Olga, the youngest child of Czar Nicholas II of Russia.
The Greek-Canadian collector returned to Greece in the mid-1960s to take over the family’s import-export business and, a few years later, opened his museum, whose gallery space and collection would expand over the decades.
The Vorres Museum consists of a complex of buildings, gardens and courtyards. Its collections span a period of 4,000 years of Greek history and art. The Museum has been donated, in the form of a cultural and artistic foundation, to the Greek state by the Vorres family.

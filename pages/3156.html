Additional Information

Alongside his younger brother, Spyros, Niarchos is Co-President and member of the Board of Directors at the Stavros Niarchos Foundation. The Stavros Niarchos Foundation (www.snf.org) is one of the world’s leading international philanthropic organizations, making grants in the areas of arts and culture, education, health and medicine, and social welfare.
Andy Warhol was commissioned to do Niarchos' portrait, and Warhol created a series based on CAT-scans of his skull.
As of March 2013 he is #554 on the list of Forbes Billionaires worldwide and #3 in Greece.
He owns Pablo Picasso's self-portrait "Yo, Picasso", which his father had bought in 1989 for $47,850,000.
He was suspected to be the anonymous buyer of Vincent van Gogh's "Self-Portrait," at a November 1998 Christie's auction.
In 1958-59 an exhibition of 72 works from the Niarchos collection traveled internationally to venues including Knoedler Galleries, the National Gallery of Canada, Ottawa, the Museum of Fine Arts, Boston, the Tate Gallery, London, and Kunsthaus Zürich.
In 2008 his net worth was estimated at GBP 850 million.
Mr. Niarchos lives intensively private.
Philip Niarchos and his brother Spyros own their late father's art collection.
Philip Niarchos is a Greek shipping heir who has a net worth of $2.6 billion.
Philip Niarchos is the eldest son of Greek shipping magnate Stavros Niarchos (1909–1996) and Eugenia Livanos (1926–1970).
Stavros Niarchos' collection is considered the "most important collections of Impressionist and modern art in private hands".
The Stavros Niarchos Foundation Cultural Center in Athens is schedule to open in 2015. Architect of the 85,000-square-meter building is Renzo Piano.

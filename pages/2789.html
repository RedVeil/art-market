Additional Information

Apart from a few works in storage or on loan, the collection is housed entirely within his basement flat in Lewisham.
As well as sharing his personal collection with the public, he also wants to provide space, within his home, to showcase further works by artists he already owns. This has led to his staging exhibitions, including 'Vicissitude' (2010) on the artist Tomas Downes.
He believes that one of the joys of collecting is the constant act of discovery. Unlike the experience of visiting a museum or gallery, where one can only view a work for a short time, he enjoys being able to discover his works in a new light on a daily basis for, he believes, it is not the works that change, but the viewer and what he brings to it.
He describes his taste in art as 'heartfelt', presenting the collection as an act of honesty, passion and integrity which reflects who he trully is.
He places great importance on maintaining a connection with the artists whose work he owns; be it through following their careers through articles or exhibitions, or by staying personally in touch with them.
He sees his collection as an archive of a certain period of time. He strongly believes that art, and the artists themselves, exist to be viewed; and, as such, welcomes people to get in touch to arrange a viewing of his personal collection.

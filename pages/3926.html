Additional Information

1. The Wonderful Fund collection was curated by Prue O'Day and Vanessa Branson, whose idea it was to set it up in 1999. The collection is co-owned by a group of 15 people. Although it was curated from 1999 to 2004, the collection still exists, although the artworks are now divided and hung in the various co-owners homes.
2. It is a collection of Contemporary art, with works dating mostly from the first five years of the century. It seeks to document the new Millenium's social, artistic, political and cultural concerns. With a reference point such as 9/11, the collection revolves around the idea of insecurity over issues of identity.
3. Owners of the collection meet twice-yearly at Branson's London home. These social events include talks by guest speakers, such as Keith Tyson and Anthony Reynolds, as the aim of these meetings is also to 'acquire the knowledge to go with the works' whose purchase was made possible through TWF.
4. They did not create the Wonderful Fund collection as an investment but as a platform for discovery and as a means of supporting young artists. They have mentioned that they might loan it permanently in the future.
5. Works in the collection, purchased by O'Day and Branson, go from £500 to just under £10,000.
She co-established and -directed the Portobello Arts Festivals with Vanessa Branson and Anatol Orient.

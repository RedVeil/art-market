Additional Information

Her desire is to work as a 'cultural diplomat' by promoting Middle Eastern art and culture, and by helping artists and art education in the Middle East, especially in Libya, which, she believes, has not yet experienced the wider Middle Eastern art boom.
She became particularly interested in street art after seeing the 'Art in the Streets' exhibition at the Los Angeles Museum of Contemporary Art (2012). She is fascinated by how street art and graffiti is used in Libya as a canvas for those artists without formal training, education or much freedom, who wish to express themselves.
She has given and moderated talks on Middle Eastern art, including on the Impact of the Arab Spring on artists at Art Basel, at the London artfair Art13, and on the cultural preservation of the past at Edge of Arabia's 'We Need to Talk' exhibition.
She has worked at the Serpentine Gallery as a consultant and at the Albion Gallery as a Director (2005-09).
She is one of a group of experts for the 'School for Creative Startups', an online course which seeks to provide the business know-how and advice that creative people need to build sustainable startups.
She is particularly interested in Middle Eastern artists, such as Susan Hefuna and Kader Attia, who manage to artistically engage with their experience of living in the West whilst having a Middle Eastern identity; something she also experiences, being part of the Libyan royal family in exile.

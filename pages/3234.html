Additional Information

In 1968, she escaped to Paris as a member of the Democratic Defense against the Greek military junta.
In 1972, Magda Kotzia was involved in the organization and publication of Mec Art Graphic in Paris, graphic art movement of Greek and international artists as an answer to US based Pop Art.
In 1974, she founded the publishing house Exantas, which now counts more than 1,000 titles, on psychology, Greek and foreign fiction, children's literature and other.
In 2012, the Macedonian Museum of Art exhibited part of a previously donated collection by Magda Kotzia that. The exhibition, called "Rue de l'Ouest 100. Mec Art Graphic 1972", hosted a collection of avant-garde French art, centered around the Paris art scene and Mrs. Kotzias first attempt in publishing at the workshop of Nikos Kessanlis and Chryssa Romanos. The exhibition was curated by Denny Zacharopoulou.
She is a well known collector and donator of artworks.
She used to be close friends with Greek artist Nikos Kessanlis.

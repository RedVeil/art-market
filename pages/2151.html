Additional Information

Although Judith and Richard Greer collect together, there also appears to be a separate 'Richard Greer Collection'.
As she shows her collection in their family home, she is not interested in 'precious' works but artworks which suit their lifestyle (she has children).
Before moving to the UK, she lived in Japan for 13 years and was Director of the Hara Museum of Contemporary Art, Tokyo (1986-1993).
Judith Greer lets non-profit organisations exhibit on the top floor of her house, which has been converted into a gallery. She also shows her own collection to visitors.
She believes in the importance of showing the artworks one has bought, and she always thinks about where she'll hang them when she buys it, saying that, if she doesn't have the space, she won't buy.
She has appeared on a number of discussion panels on art-collecting and the contemporary art-market. She has also been a guest lecturer for Sotheby's MAAB course (MA in Art Business).
She is particularly interested in the emerging art market in the Middle East and is involved in a number of projects and institutions, mostly in the UAE, where she has given talks during Ladies Day at Art Dubai.
She is passionate about nurturing young artists, and fostering their work and reputation

For general enquiries concerning Michael Audain`s art collection contact Barbara Binns: bbinns@polyhomes.com / 604 871-4207
In December 2012 Michael Audain and his wife Yoshiko Kurasawa signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) with the Resort Municipality of Whistler, to commit to their plans of establishing a small museum that will display their impressive private art collection. In May 2012 he revealed the plans for the museum which stated that the museum will be 55,000 square feet big.
Michael Audain collects together with his wife Yoshiko Karasawa.
Michael Audin owns the stongest collection of Emily Carr in Canada.
Mr Michael Audain's Audain Foundation for the Visual Arts gifted $2.5-million to the Museum of Anthropology at the University of British Columbia (UBC) in 2009.
To date, Mr Michael Audain has given more than $6,000,000 to the Vancouver Art Gallery, making him the gallery's biggest individual donor. To visual-arts institutions in Canada, he has given nearly $17 million

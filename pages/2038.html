Additional Information

In 2004 in Chiba just outside of Tokyo, Daisuke Miyatsu built the Dream House to serve as both his home and as part of his private collection. It was designed by French artist Dominique Gonzalez-Foerster and much of the interior design features were made specially by world-famous artists. The wallpaper pattern was created by the South Korean artist Jung Yeon Doo. The stairs leading to a tall mirror were designed by Yayoi Kusama and a chair by Thai artist Surasi Kusolwong. The garden was designed by Japanese artist Shimabuku.
He called himself a salary-man collector on different public occasions. He works at a PR company in Tokyo and collects art works with his salary.
He is a part-time lecturer on art collecting at the Kyoto University of Art and Design.
Daisuke Miyatsu has started inviting artists to use his body as their canvas by designing tattoos for him, including Keiichi Tanamai, Ryan Gander, Djordje Ozbolt, and Daido Moriyama.

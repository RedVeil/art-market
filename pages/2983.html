Additional Information

An education centre at New York’s New Museum is named after her and her late husband: The Pauline and Constantine Karpidas Education Center.
In 2009, she auctioned the Andy Warhol painting 200 One Dollar Bills, which she had bought with her late husband Constantine Karpidas. The painting realized $43.8 million, it made more than 100 times what Ms. Karpidas had paid in 1986.
In 2012, Karpidas donated a vast sum of money to The University Of Manchester. This includes the prestigious Karpidas Excellence Scholarship.
Karpidas was introduced to the art world in the 1970s by Athens art dealer Alexander Iolas.
Ms. Karpidas opened the gallery called 'Hydra Workshop' for the purpose of sharing her art. Nothing is for sale and the space is not leased out for other commercial activities.
Ms. Karpidas works closely with British gallerist Sadie Cole. Sadie Cole is an important part of The Hydra workshop, the yearly organized exhibition project on the Greek island Hydra with works from Ms. Karpidas collection. They publish a catalogue each year since 2000. (All catalogues are available here: http://www.sadiecoles.com/books/the-hydra-workshops)
Pauine Karpidas is the widow of Greek shipping tycoon Constantine Karpidas.
Pauline Karpidas is an importerd art collector, gallery owner and socialite.

Additional Information

He collects mainly artworks connected to the Papunya movement, a contemporary art movement that started at the Papunya Aboriginal Community in 1971.
He has been donating artworks to several institutions and galleries.
He started working as a teacher in Papunya, a small Indigenous Australian community, where he got introduced to contemporary Aboriginal art and started collecting it.
In 2006 Arthur Papadimitriou received the highest French award, Ordre des Arts et des Lettres|Chevalier De Ordre des Arts et des Lettres, by the Minister of Culture and Communication, for his contribution to French and Australian culture.
The Arthur Papadimitriou Collection has brought some significant paintings by indigenous artists to Christie’s auction of Contemporary Art, that is held in Melbourne.
The Wilin Centre for Indigenous Arts and Cultural Development at the Victorian College of the Arts (The University of Melbourne) has an annual award titled “The Papadimitriou Inspirational Award” for students at the college.

Additional Information

After moving to Montreal, he began collecting figurative artworks which he found accessible. By going to more museums, galleries and Contemporary art exhibitions, he started to be attracted to more challenging works.
As a writer, he has collaborated with the sculptor Michel Goulet in writing texts carved into monumental sculptures, including the installation ‘Nulle part/Ailleurs’ set on the shores of the river Magog in Sherbrooke.
Having too many artworks for his own home, he used to display part of his collection in his former company, Mackenzie Gervais', offices.
He believes collecting photography is harder than collecting paintings, as it takes more effort to keep up to date with new trends and with the progress of the medium. He is drawn to Contemporary photography's 'culture of the ephemeral' because its actuality.
He has owned in the past up to 803 artworks by 363 artists, before his major donation of 390 works to the Musée des beaux-arts de Sherbrooke; 125 photographs to the Musée d'art de Joliette and another 125 œuvres to various institutions, including about twenty to l'Université de Sherbrooke.
He is keen on edgy, confrontational work and carries out extensive research on the artworks, looking for artists consistent in quality and for art on the cutting edge. As well as collecting Contemporary photography, he also collects works by artists integral to the history of photography.
He regularly reinvents his collection with new acquisitions, rotates the artworks on his walls and looks out for new talent in universities.
He started by collecting works which charmed him; then works which fascinating him, and finally works which stirred him. He now looks for works which make him ask questions, which intrigue him.
His parents were collectors before him, but it was at university that he became drawn to Contemporary art, regularly visiting his university's art gallery, run at the time by Claude Lafleur.

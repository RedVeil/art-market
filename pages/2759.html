Additional Information

Although he sees the importance of commercial galleries' role in generating visual culture, he wants his exhibition space, Raven Row, to show what art can do when it is not constrained by commercial considerations.
As part of his involvement with Raven Row, he co-curates the exhibitions held there and sponsors an artist residency programme.
He co-curated the 'A Skvader' Exhibition at the Norwich Castle Museum & Art Gallery (2011).
He has taken part in talks and conferences on contemporary art collecting, including the 'TODAY + TOMORROW' event at the White Cube (2012). He has also been involved with educational programmes, having been a visiting tutor for the MFA Curating at Goldsmiths and spoken for a course on Collecting at the Whitechapel Gallery (2006).
He is not a big collector, for he wants to be able to enjoy the work he owns, rather than possess it for the sake of ownership.
He used to direct the art project-space ‘38 Langham Street', London.

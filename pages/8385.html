Additional Information

In 2017, David Geffen gave $150 million to the Los Angeles County Museum of Art for a gut renovation of the hall named after himself. It was also the largest single donation received by the museum in its history to date.
David Geffen’s yacht, Rising Sun, is 454 feet long, employs a crew of 48, and is one of the largest yachts in the world, according to Super Yacht Fan.
Geffen is a college dropout with dyslexia.
“Free Man in Paris,” written and recorded by Joni Mitchell for her 1974 album “Court and Spark” (on the Asylum label), was a character sketch of Mr. Geffen in those years, and it captured him in all his contradictions.
Founded in 1986, the David Geffen Foundation provides grants to people affected by HIV/AIDS and to promote civil liberties including LBGTQI+ rights, the arts, Jewish causes, and health care. While the Geffen Foundation is a sizable organisation, it maintains a low public profile, does not operate a website and employs just one formal staff member.
In 1994, Geffen arranged a swap with the Iranian government to provide them with a precious Persian manuscript in exchange for selling a de Kooning from the Tehran Museum’s legendary collection.
Geffen was born in Brooklyn, the son of a Polish father and Russian mother. His mother, Batya, owned a corset store; his father, Abraham, was a pattern maker in the garment trade.
In 2018, Geffen acquired four paintings by Mark Grotjahn as well as a six-panel canvas by David Hockney.
In 2016, Geffen donated $100 million to Museum of Modern Art in NYC.

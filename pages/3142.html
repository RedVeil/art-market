Additional Information

Although often described as a collector, she however calls herself an 'enabler', which she believes better describes the desire which lies behind her art engagement: a focus not on ownership, but on the desire to make things happen.
As a collector, she places great importance on conversations and on her relationship to artists and seeks to understand their ideas, how a work of art fits into their thinking and development.
Her foundation LUMA has worked with a number of museums and art institutions, including the Kunsthallen in Zurich and Basel; the Fotomuseum Winterthur; the Palais de Tokyo in Paris; the Venice Biennale and the Serpentine Gallery, London.
Her interest in art was first encouraged by her grandmother, who collected works by Pablo Picasso, Jean Arp, Fernand Léger, and ­Georges Braque, and who was also friends with a number of artists.
She has a strong relationship with the artists she supports, and either funds their work directly or buys artworks from their studios. She rarely re-sells works.
She has supported the careers of a number of artists, including Fischli, Weiss, Fischer, Aitken, Philippe Parreno and Douglas Gordon.
She is in the process of developing a project, Le Parc des Ateliers in Arles, France, which will stand as a cultural and arts centre.

Additional Information

He also set up the Bag Factory, Johannesburg, with the artist David Koloane.
He began by collecting British prints, dating from 1790 to 1930,as a was of learning about painting. He has since sold this collection to the Koriyama City Museum of Art, Japan, after displaying them at the Fitzwilliam Museum, Cambridge in 1985.
He has spoken, alongside his co-founder Sir Anthony Caro, at the Triangle Network Conference (2011).
He is known as the 'workshop man' for having pioneered the concept of art workshops on a global scale, particularly through the Triangle Network which he set up with Sir Anthony Caro.
He was treasurer, then chairman, of the Institute of Contemporary Arts in the 1970s.
Most of the paintings he owns were bought directly from the artists themselves, rather than being carefully selected through a gallery.

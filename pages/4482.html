Additional Information

Although he undertakes detailed research on the artists whom he collects, he also believes that, quoting the collector Georges Ortiz, 'One can know too much and feel too little.'
As well as having his own private art collection, Jack Lazare also collects artworks for his company Vision 2000.
He has admited to making many impulse art purchases in London, Paris, and New York as well as in Toronto and Montreal.
His early artistic taste centered on the works of Käthe Kollwitz, Alex Katz, Jean-Paul Lemieux, and Christopher Pratt; as well as on the art of Lucian Freud, Giacometti, and Edward Hopper, of whom he owns several etchings and lithographs.
Jack and Harriet Lazare began collecting representational painting in the 1960s. After discovering works by the artist Julia Margaret Cameron at MOMA in 1999, they turned their collecting interests to photography and now own over ten of her works. Their collection has since gone on to include Canadian and international Contemporary photography.

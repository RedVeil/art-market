Additional Information

A number of Chinese paintings in his collection are displayed at the London Business School, where he is a governor, as he no longer has room for them at home.
Ha acted as Senior Adviser for the British Museum’s exhibition 'Word into Art: Artists of the Modern Middle East' (2006).
He has also spoken on one of ARCO's 2009 programmes on ‘A Patron’s Perspective on the Art of Emerging Markets’.
He thinks before purchasing an artwork, as he looks at a piece in depth and seeks to impose an aesthetic unity within his collection.
He wrote 'Art of the Middle East: Modern and Contemporary Art of the Arab World and Iran' as a way of engaging, and dispelling, prejudices and stereotypes about the Middle East, as he believes culture plays a crucial role in bridging such gaps.
His interest in Chinese art began on a trip to Beijing and Shanghai in 1994 whilst he was still an MBA student.
His parents were also art collectors and a particular artwork of theirs, a small painting by the Lebanese artist Mustafa Farroukh, made a lasting impression on him.

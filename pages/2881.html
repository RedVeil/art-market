Additional Information

He decided to buy a new piece of art for each new dependence of his bank in Greece.
He left his collection to Alpha Bank. With the merger of Alpha Bank and Emboriki Bank in 2012 a great new collection of 5,600 works was established. He still collects privately.
His daughter is the contemporary Greek artist Daphne Costopoulos.
His net worth is $ 425.5 million.
In 1997 he supported the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York financially for the realization of the exhibition 'The Glory of Byzantium: Art and Culture of the Middle Byzantine Era'.
Yannis Costopoulos is chairman and executive director of one of the largest banks on the Balkan peninsula – Alpha Bank, which was founded by his grandfather 160 years ago. He is also an owner of the Athens Hilton Hotel.
Yannis Costopoulos is chairman of the J. F. Costopoulos Foundation, a non-profit charitable institution that was founded in 1979, on the occasion of the centenary of the Alpha Bank, in memory of its founder John F. Costopoulos by its then Chairman, the late Spyros Costopoulos and his wife Eurydice. The foundation supports the safeguard and promotion of the Greek culture, literature and the arts both in Greece and abroad.

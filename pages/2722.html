David Martinez sold Francis Bacon`s “Portrait of George Dyer Talking" for $70 million in 2014, which was an auction record for a single canvas by the Bacon. He bought in for $12 million more than 5 years ago.
He is infamously difficult to contact and little is known about his businesses and art collection.
In 2006 the painting No.5, 1948 by Jackson Pollock was sold to Guzmán for $151.8 million.
Martinez Guzman has been subject to court cases regarding his purchase and sale of certain important pieces of art, including in 2009, regarding a 1913 sculpture by Constantin Brancusi, "Mademoiselle Pogany", which he supposedly bought through his alter-ego company, Studio Capital, when an agreement had already been made between the sellers and another buyer.

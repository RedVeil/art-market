Additional Information

As an advisor at Frahm Ltd., one of Nicolai Frahm's first major clients was Frank Cohen, who, although he'd been collecting Modern Art for several decades, came to him for advice on collecting contemporary works. They have since co-founded the Dairy Art Centre.
Brothers Nicolai and Michael Frahm are thinking of opening their own museum or foundation, possibly in London, thus following in their father Flemming Frahm's footsteps, who established a family foundation in Copenhagen, Galerie Frahm,, which opened in the mid 90s.
He has publicly supported the work of Ai Weiwei.
When asked what piece advice is essential for collectors looking to collect contemporary art, Nicolai Frahm firmly believes that quality is better than quantity, preferring to buy only a few masterpieces rather than a number of 'mediocre' works.
Works from his collection were exhibited at London's The Hospital Club (2006).

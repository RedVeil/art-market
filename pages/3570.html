Additional Information

He only buys artworks after having discussed with his own informal advisory panel, which includes a University professor, a restorer and a dealer.
He purchased John Constable's 'View of the Stour' for £6.7m, and Thomas Gainsborough's 'Peasants Going To Market' for £3.5m.
He sold Edvard Munch's 'Girls on a Bridge' at Sotheby's in 2008 for $30.8 million (£15m), having bought it for $7.7m in 1996.
His collecting Irish art comes not from any personal or national connections, as is often the case in the collecting of Irish works, but is based purely on the quality of the artworks in question.
His collection shows Irish art not in isolation, as is so often the case, but within its broader context, side by side with their English and French contemporaries.

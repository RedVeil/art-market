Additional Information

He began by collecting Pop Art, with works such as Warhol multiples. Once these became more expensive, he started collecting younger artists. He finds it exciting being able to see them develop and grow into their own.
He believes that the British Art world is being held back by the media's continuing interest in the YBA movement; an interest which stops it from looking to younger British and European artists.
He believes that the major International Art Fairs are a fairer reflection of how Contemporary Art is priced; more so than any auction.
He has given talks on collecting, including at the Manchester Contemporary (2010).
He is particularly keen on the post-war COBRA art movement, especially on the Dutch artist Karel Appel, and on the Contemporary Art scene.
Hugo and Carla Brown regularly attend VIP openings of Contemporary Art shows in Europe, the USA and Mexico.

Additional Information

Franck Hénot and Danny Therrien started their collection when they met about 12 years ago and, like many collectors, began by purchasing works on paper, often less expensive than works on canvas.
The Hénot-Therrien collection is due to be donated to the Musée de Joliette, Québec after their deaths.
Their collection is mostly composed of artworks by artists from Québec from 20th and 21st c., often by artists influenced by the abstract expressionist movement of the 1950s.
They see collecting as a way of life and themselves as the temporary guardians of the artworks, whose responsibility it is to guarantee its sustainability.

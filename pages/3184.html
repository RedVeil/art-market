Additional Information

He also owns a collection of over 300 cartoons.
He auctioned off 150 works from his collection at Christie's on 27th-28th June 2011. The lots included works by Marc Chagall, Claude Monet, Andy Warhol, Edouard Vuillard and Pierre Bonnard.
He has started to think about the future of his collection. As well as asking his sons to each select a dozen works which they would like to inherit, he has promised six works to both the Fitzwilliam Museum in Cambridge and the Ashmolean Museum in Oxford, to be donated after his death. The rest of the collection will go to his wife, Lady Archer.
He opened an art gallery in Mayfair, the Archer Gallery, in 1969 which specialised in modern art, with a number of works by the artist Leon Underwood. It closed two years later.
He opened an exhibition on the woodcarver Thomas Wallis at the Louth Museum in May 2012.
He was a business partner of the recently closed Neffe Degandt Gallery, Mayfair.
Most of his collection is housed in the Archers' Cambridge home, with the rest mostly on loan or in their Majorca villa.
The first auction of works from his collection took place in 1998, during which he sold most of his collection of works by Andy Warhol which included 42 oil paintings and 328 acrylics. His decision to sell them was based on his having collected the works for 'purely commercial reasons', having always intended to sell them.

Additional Information

In May 2011, Jan Mol's fortune was estimated at around £450m.
Mol’s Place was a converted Grade II listed building with a rich artistic history, built in 1851. It was originally a scenic painter’s workshop for the Royal Opera House and for theatres located around Covent Garden.
'Mol's Place' was both Jan Mol's home and a contemporary art space where he held exhibitions and events to promote the work of emerging artists. Its closing event was in October 2011, after which Jan Mol was to move.

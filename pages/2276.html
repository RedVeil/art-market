Additional Information

Her collection focusses on contemporary Iranian art which gives her a sense of connection to her roots, a 'nostalgic art'.
Maryam and Edward Eisler have set up a foundation to provide funding for curatorial posts, acquisitions and exhibitions relating to contemporary Iranian art, most especiaaly at the British Museum and Tate.
Maryam Homayoun Eisler can be reached via her art advisor Fariba Farshad (fariba.farshad@candlestar.co.uk)
She has written for international art magazines and exhibition catalogues, and was, amongst other things, curatorial director for 'Different Sames: New Perspectives in Contemporary Iranian Art' (Thames & Hudson, 2009).
She is also closely involved with the British Museum’s Middle Eastern patrons group.
She is keen to get involved in publication, curatorial support, purchases, and even research; and would love to help institutions set up libraries of Middle Eastern contemporary and modern art.
She is loath to part with pieces in her collection, having only every sold three pieces due to their not fitting in.
She was involved in the realization of the New York Chelsea Art Museum's 'Iran Inside Out' (2009) exhibition.
Their foundation supports the post of Eisler Curator of Modern and Contemporary Middle Eastern Art at the British Museum.

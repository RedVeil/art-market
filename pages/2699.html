Additional Information

Between 1978 and 1989 he collected drawings by unknown and established Greek artists. Between 1990 and 2000 he focused on European artists. By the time the Frissiras Museum opened its doors, Frissiras was able to present a panorama of Greek and European art.
In 1972 he opened his law office in Athens through which he later funded his cultural work.
The Frissiras Collection participates at DCA, a project that aims to digitise contemporary art objects from 12 European countries and make them accessible to the wider public. PACKED vzw - Centre of Expertise in Digital Heritage in Belgium is responsible fort the project. (http://www.dca-project.eu/).
The Frissiras Museum is the only Museum for Contemporary European Painting in Greece.
The Frissiras Museum was founded by the Vlassis Frissiras family, which is also responsible for the administration of the foundation, which consists of a non-profit organisation. The museum houses the private collection of its founder.
Vlassis Frissiras is an attorney by profession but passionate about art.

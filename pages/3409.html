Additional Information

George and Maura McClelland opened a gallery in Belfast in the 1960s, which dealt in both historic and Contemporary art, including Islamic and African art, and Russian icons. It especially showcased Contemporary Irish art and encouraged artists from Northern Ireland in particular. Through this, George McClelland became the friend, and agent, of the artists Dan O’Neill, Gerard Dillon, F E McWilliam and Colin Middleton, among others.
He auctioned off parts of his collection in May and December 2005.
He made his first purchase, a drawing which he still owns, at the age of 12.
In 1969, the McClellands established the McClelland Fine Art Award for final year Diploma students at the Ulster College of Art and Design. This was later picked up and continued by the Arts Council of Northern Ireland.
They retired to to the Isle of Man in 1986, but return regularly to Ireland.

Additional Information

By purchasing The House of Approximate Odds, he rescued this 1930s building in the centre of Catterick Racecourse from being demolished. In 2001, he made it the home of an installation by Paul Etienne Lincoln, of whose work Greville is a big fan.
Greville lives in a converted Victorian church in Brough Park near Richmond, North Yorkshire, which houses both his private collection as well as hosting various public exhibitions.
He has built up a huge archive of material relating to his collection, such as books, catalogues, PV invites and correspondence with artists, which he describes as “the coalface of my collection” (Corridor8, p.7).

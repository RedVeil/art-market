Additional Information

Every year she sets herself a budget with which to purchase artworks.
Her first art purchase was in 1976, when she bought a small lithograph from an itinerant artist in front of the MoMa in New York.
She advises collectors to take their time and to visit artists' centres and exhibitions, and to let themselves be seduced by a work of art.
She bought a sculpture in 2006 for 1 800$, now estimated to be worth 10 000$.
She doesn't see herself as a collector, seeing the artworks she owns as her most intimate possessions; the story of her life and of her meetings, friendships and way of life. She doesn't see buying art as an investment but as the desire to live with an artwork, and the priviledge of being able to do so.
She has given a number of talks on collecting Contemporary art.
